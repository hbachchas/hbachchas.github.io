I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Roorkee. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Balasubramanian Raman. I received my masters in Computer Engineering from National Institute of Technology, Kurukshetra, where I was advised by Prof. Gyanendra Verma on object detection and emotion recognition. Prior to that, I worked with Newgen, on BPM and ECM related products. Before joining Newgen, I received my bachelors in Computer Science and Engineering from Harcourt Butler Technical University, Kanpur.

Research Interests

My present research revolves around video analysis, with a focus on understanding — motion, action recognition, generative models, and manifold learning. I am trying to develop algorithms that can understand video content in the absence of labels. The primary focus is on video representations. My broad areas of interest are:

  • Computer Vision
  • Machine Learning
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Cognitive Architectures
